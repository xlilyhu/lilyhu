introduction

in the spring semester of 2019, i was introduced to the blackstone through the blackstone future women innovators program. through it, i visited and experienced the fast-paced life of nyc for the first time in over fifteen years, learned what blackstone innovations is all about, and had one of the best bowls of ramen here. later in july of 2019, my mentor reached out to me to apply to the summer internship. fast-forward to september, i was walking out of my finance class when i got a call from blackstone telling me that i've received an offer!

my team!


sooooo... what the heck is blackstone innovations? let's break it down into sections:

blackstone itself is the world's largest private equity/alternative assets management and financial services firm. there's many teams that operate under the blackstone umbrella, and they've been involved in some pretty neat deals (see oatly & ancestry). if you're wondering what exactly private equity is, check out this video.

blackstone innovations (now rebranded as blackstone technology and innovations) is the team that supports all the stuff going on behind the scenes. they're the people that brings efficiency within deal flow, and makes the lives of the deal teams and lps (limited partners) a lot easier, and innovations is constantly implementing new technologies that brings it to the front of industry competitors (looking at you, kkr, apollo, and goldman sachs!).

there are many teams that reside within blackstone innovations, from engagement teams, to product teams. this summer, i was a product business analyst operating under the product management team, which focuses on products (applications) to be used by the deal flow teams. specifically, within the pm team, i worked with the ux team on its rebrand efforts, diving straight into three design sprints that challenged me as a creative and as a student.

this is my fantastic team!


project!

my task this summer was to completely redesign the original blackstone investor portal suite, which includes numerous applications within the portal. i worked with a team of two other interns, and with the guidance of our senior and junior mentors, we embarked on a series of three design sprints, and in the following three weeks, re-iterated and began our user testing sessions. our three sprints revolved around forms and inputs, grids and dashboards, and global components and card designs - i led the final sprint!

industry research

this section comprises of in depth industry research, where my team pulled ui graphics from other designs found on behance and dribble, and observed competitor designs as well. from this, we would jump into lightning demos, finding industry competitors that we thought would be useful, then backing our research with compelling articles. we'd also participate in 'expert interviews,' with employees who were familiar with and had already worked on the portals, to get their understanding and feedback on what we could implement or improve on. then , we would complete a 'how might we' exercise, where my teammates and i would come up with the issues that we wanted to focus on throughout our design sprints. because of nda agreements, i can't fully go into what the project specs were - contact me if you'd like to know more!

analytics

in this portion, my team and i used google analytics to pull data from current portal usage, mainly focusing on user behavior and events. because we wanted to minimize redundancy among our designs, we chose to hone in on user interactions (events such as number of clicks) and user time spent on site. from there, we would also pull data and analyze it through tableau.

design & iterations

one of the most fun parts of the design sprint, yet simultaneously so frustrating. my team and i used adobe xd to mock up the sites and to simulate our new re-design. throughout the week, we would continue to tweak and improve on our designs, while following the rebrand guidelines that were already in place. at the end of the week, we would have a design review with the front end developers, and employees who currently work on the portal, to get their feedback. the user review sessions were so helpful, because through their feedback, we were able to vastly improve our designs. to prepare for the user testing session of our design project, my team and i worked diligently to prototype our designs so that they resembled that of a working portal, complete with hover features, clickable expandable cards, and folder implementation (and much, much more!).

user testing

the user testing portion focused on grasping a user's interaction with our current designs. we conducted a/b testing, by testing users on the original brand investor portal, and then tested those same users on our re-design prototypes. in these user testing sessions, we randomly selected employees, and later, senior level execs (mds, the cto john stecher, the cto clientwide services rob wisniewski, and others!!!!) and guided them through timed tasks to see how quickly they'd be able to identify or navigate our prototypes. through this, we were able to more easily determine places where our prototypes fell short and how to continuously improve on them!

other cool stuff i got to work on!

diversity and inclusion

through bxti, i joined the diversity and inclusion committee - led by meiya chang and priya kankanhalli (inspiring people). i teamed up with another intern, rohan, and we wanted to uphold the 'colleague' and 'community' goals of the d&i committee. largely inspired by stanford's 'what i wish my professor knew' study, i wanted to implement a survey that would drive transparency in the workplace and foster conversations to help bxti develop a more inclusive, open space. i'm excited to see where this project goes - meiya and priya have mentioned that they are going to take this one step further and call it a campaign, where they'll also make a video having different employees read the anonymous responses.

pro-bono project

my intern class was the first to implement these pro-bono projects. all the interns partipated in one of 10 projects, and i was on the start small think big: small business finance project. here, i helped the non-profit start small think big, an organization with the mission to support women/bipoc entrepreneurs and small businesses. through this, my team of six and i were able to push our recommendations on different institutional seeds, accelerators, and incubators to partner with and potentially recommend their clients to. our other recommendations revolved around how to manage budgetting and financing for these small businesses.

chummings!

because this summer was all remote, it became that much more difficult to meet other interns. our solution? randomize and pair interns together weekly for an informal coffee chat. sri, another intern, and i worked together on this project. i focused on pairing students through randomizing them on excel, while she planned the themes per week. this was an incredible experience that allowed me to work more closely with the hr team, and meet so many incredible interns across different business groups.
